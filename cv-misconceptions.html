Common Misconceptions regarding Computer Vision

Breaking the fallacies of Computer Vision

Computer Vision beholds a new way of evolution in the world of Technology and Artificial Intelligence. With more enterprises being enthusiastic towards the spectrum of this limb of AI and its advancing vogue, a pool of murmur encompassing both light and dark hues among the public opinion is being generated where people have their queries and suspicions in abundance, that needs to be addressed.



Common Misconceptions

  • Misconception 1: Computer Vision exceeds the excellence of Human Eye



    One can not deny the fact that CV has come a long way in controlling simulations similar to that of a man. From distinguishing different components to characterizing them in the desired format, CV has acquired higher efficiency with updated convolutional neural networks.

    But as Fei-Fei Li (an American computer scientist ) stated in her 2015 TED Talks on image distinction that " Even the smartest machines are still blind ”, Computer Vision can end up having several omissions, especially with inputs that consist more backstory, complex notions that need to be addressed by the intellect of an individual at the end of the day.


  • Misconception 2: Computer Vision encourages robot dominance

    This might sound like a perfect plot for the next blockbuster sci-fi movie and many viewers show their skepticisms regarding the same, which is somehow understandable with the broadening significance of CV and AI in general ( thanks to the movies that portray a giant mechanical body with a huge lens that scans everything with random targets and red lines ).

    It is also important to note that mankind developed these applications like CV for their very own convenience and having more regulation systemically. There will be circumstances where these lenses will rely on the timely inputs and updates initiated by humans themselves and we still got a long way to go in developing human-free mechanisms.


  • Misconception 3: Computer Vision puts privacy at stake

    CV is today stepping out of its boundaries and is being adopted by diverse types of agencies and organizations, including public platforms, law enforcement, and security-driven. This puts many into a delusion that CV is used to intrude in their privacy.



    On the other hand, as Forbes puts forward the scenario of India having 242,000 children going missing in the past 5 years and how facial recognition technology of CV helped in identifying 3,000 of them during their examinations with the system, a full-fledged structure of amendments to ensure prudent use of CV with privacy at haven.


  • Misconception 4: Computer Vision will be 'eating' jobs

    Apparently, the capability of Computer Vision has reached a point where it can be used to perform a task that probably takes a whole team of intellectuals with hours of work. This makes many worried that CV might end up reducing the ratio of employment.

    Undoubtedly, CV has the capability to do multiple jobs simultaneously but it is still a mechanism working on the structure laid by human hands itself. With the advancement in CV, the technology is also unlocking the doors for new vibrant opportunities and fields of excellence with promising amounts of yields for people to seek a scope into.


  • Misconception 5: It takes fortunes to establish Computer Vision



    Establishing a CV takes as much undertaking as hiring an entirely equipped team of professionals. Both require a well-organized approach, a source of planning, and a good portion of revenue. Many industries especially SMEs are still in an illusion that technology is only meant for ginormous MNCs and hence resorts to traditional methods.

    However, expending on the CV comes with the assurance of a one-time investment that will give outputs in return for years, without any fluctuations inefficiency. Adding to this, choosing the right type of automated system from credible sources for your business model to generate more value, to increase productivity, and scale your business on the next level, while keeping the demand in mind can make CV affordable and equally accessible to all and avoiding the strain over prevailing budget.


Forthcoming

In the end, one needs to realize that Computer Vision was brought into existence in the favor of mankind, and updates in Computer Vision will aid the forthcoming of technology, advances of profitable projects for humans, and different sectors in commerce, which can be best described in the following lines by renowned American computer scientist Alan Kay —

“Some people worry that artificial intelligence will make us feel inferior, but then, anybody in his right mind should have an inferiority complex every time he looks at a flower.”
